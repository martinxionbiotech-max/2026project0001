Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q1: What is a blood moon?
A blood moon occurs during a total lunar eclipse when the moon appears reddish due to Earth’s shadow.

Q2: What is a solar eclipse?
A solar eclipse occurs when the moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, partially or fully blocking sunlight.

Q3: How do I check if the events are visible in my country?
Use the dropdown above or the automatic location detection to see visibility and local times.

Q4: When is the next blood moon or solar eclipse?
Use the checkers to see exact local and UTC times for your country.

Q5: Why does the moon appear red during a blood moon?
During a total lunar eclipse, Earth’s atmosphere scatters sunlight, filtering out blue light and letting red light reach the moon, giving it a reddish hue.

Q6: Can I see these events from any country?
Visibility depends on location. Some countries may not see the eclipse. Use the checkers above to verify.
